# Business Model

## Honest assessment

This document replaces earlier projections ($720M ARR, unicorn in Year 3) that were speculative and not grounded in data.

---

## What we're selling

A local-first AI orchestration tool for developers with a subscription model.

---

## Pricing

Network effects pricing: infrastructure cost per user decreases with scale. That saving is passed on directly.

| Plan | Launch price | At 100K users |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| Free | $0 / 3 bots, 100 generations/month | $0 |
| Individual | $29/month | ~$18/month |
| Pro | $79/month | ~$48/month |
| Team (10 seats) | $299/month | ~$180/month |

Price updates are automatic and public. The formula is published so users can verify it.

---

## Revenue model

Simple SaaS subscriptions. No ads, no data selling, no upsells.

---

## Realistic projections

These are scenarios, not forecasts. They require validating that people actually pay for this.

**Conservative (product-market fit is hard to find):**
- Year 1: $50K–200K ARR (100–700 paying users)
- Year 2: $200K–1M ARR with referral growth
- Year 3: $1M–5M ARR if retention is strong

**Moderate (clear product-market fit):**
- Year 1: $500K–2M ARR
- Year 2: $3M–8M ARR
- Year 3: $10M–25M ARR

The difference between these scenarios is entirely whether developers find the tool genuinely useful enough to pay for and recommend. That is unknown until we have real users.

---

## What needs to be true for this to work

1. Developers value privacy enough to pay for local execution (unvalidated)
2. The output quality is good enough that people use it daily (partially validated by benchmarks)
3. Network effects pricing is a meaningful acquisition driver (untested)
4. Retention is high enough that LTV > CAC (unknown)

Each of these is a hypothesis. The right approach is to test them cheaply before scaling.

---

## Competitive risk

GitHub Copilot and Cursor have large user bases, editor integration, and significant investment. If either adds strong privacy guarantees, the main differentiator of Resonance weakens.

The honest answer is: build fast, find the users who care about privacy today, and stay close to what they actually need.
